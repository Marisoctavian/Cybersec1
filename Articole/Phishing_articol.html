Phishing Attacks and How to Avoid Them

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber-attack where hackers try to steal your sensitive data (passwords, credit card numbers) by tricking you into clicking fake links or opening fraudulent emails.

Common Types of Phishing Attacks

  • 📧 **Email Phishing** – Fake emails that appear to be from banks, social media, or companies.
  • 📱 **SMS Phishing (Smishing)** – Text messages with dangerous links.
  • 🎣 **Spear Phishing** – Targeted attacks that use personal information.
  • 🎭 **Website Spoofing** – Fake websites that look like real ones to steal login credentials.

How to Spot a Phishing Email

  • ⚠ **Suspicious sender** – Check the email address.
  • 🔗 **Hover over links** before clicking. If they look strange, don’t open them!
  • 💬 **Grammar mistakes & urgency** – Hackers try to make you panic.
  • 🔒 **Legit companies never ask for passwords via email**.

What to Do If You Fall for Phishing?

🚨 If you clicked on a phishing link, do this immediately:

  • 🛡 Change your passwords.
  • 🔍 Enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
  • 🕵 Report the phishing attempt to your IT department or email provider.
